Nothing important catches you off guard.

Heads-Up is a single place that anticipates everything in your life that needs lead time, from bills and birthdays to renewals, maintenance, and follow-ups, and surfaces each one early enough that you can actually plan for it.

Coming soon toGoogle Play Coming soon toApp Store
The problem it owns

Life-admin lives in a dozen silos.

Birthdays sit in your contacts, bills in your banking app, the car service in your head, the loan document in your inbox. None of them warns you early enough to act. They ping you the day it is due, when it is already too late to buy the flowers or avoid the late fee.

Lead time is the unmet need

Existing tools remind you at the moment, not ahead of it. A reminder that fires the day of is useless when the thing needed preparation.

The silos are the friction

No single place holds bills and birthdays and car maintenance and document follow-ups, so nothing can give you one honest picture of what is coming.

The cost is real

When one slips it is not just annoying. It is a late fee, a lapsed policy, a missed birthday, a credit-score ding. Real money and real relationships.

The shift

What if everything that mattered lived in one place, and warned you early enough to actually do something?

You stop holding it all in your head. The promise is peace of mind, not another to-do list to feel guilty about.

Positioning

Anticipation, not task management.

This is the frame that sets Heads-Up apart from a crowded field of reminder and to-do apps. It does not capture what is on your plate. It tells you what is coming, with enough runway to prepare.

The offering

One clean model for everything worth a heads-up.

Everything worth surfacing shares one shape: something needs attention, early enough to prepare. Where they differ is recurrence. Heads-Up captures that in three cadence types.

1

One-time

Discrete things like sending flowers or submitting loan documents. When done, a one-time can spawn a follow-up (submitted docs, then "revision needed, resubmit in two weeks") so the thread continues instead of ending.

2

Recurrent

Calendar-anchored, time-bound obligations like paying the credit-card bill before the 4th of every month, where a miss carries financial or credit consequences. Surfaced on schedule, every cycle.

3

Retriggered

Completion-anchored recurrence like refilling engine oil every six months. The next instance is calculated from when you actually did it, so a late completion re-anchors correctly instead of nagging you right away.

Why it is different

The moat is the synthesis, not a single feature.

One model unifies what is currently split across a task app, a car app, and a document vault. On top of that sit the parts the rest of the field handles poorly.

The synthesis itself

One home for bills, birthdays, maintenance, and document follow-ups. Not any single feature, but the unification, is what makes it defensible.

Notes and lineage

Every cadence carries a running thread of notes, so you always see the context and history and never lose the reason it mattered.

Follow-up chaining

The one-time that spawns a follow-up keeps a thread alive when a task is not truly finished. A genuine gap in the field.

Retriggered cadence

Completion-anchored repeats prove Heads-Up understands real-world recurrence more deeply than the generalists, who bury or botch it.

The unified view

Everything coming up, sorted by when you need to act.

The "all in one place" promise made visible: every type and every domain in one calm list, ordered by when you need to prepare rather than by when it is due. It reads as calm anticipation, not a dense task list.
